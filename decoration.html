Chinese New Year Decorations

Red Lanterns 红灯笼

Red lanterns symbolize good fortune, happiness, vitality, prosperity, and warding off evil spirits, making them central to celebrations like the New Year and Lantern Festival.

Paper Cuttings 窗花

Chinese paper-cutting, known as Jianzhi (剪纸), is one of China’s most cherished folk arts, carrying over 1,500 years of history. As the Lunar New Year approaches, this delicate craft transforms homes into vibrant galleries of red and white, symbolizing luck, prosperity, and the joy of a fresh start. Jianzhi are traditionally pasted onto windows, earning them the nickname "window flowers." When the winter sun shines through the paper, it casts beautiful, complex shadows into the home, creating a warm and festive atmosphere.

Spring Couplets 春联

Chinese spring couplets (春联) are poetic verses, typically red paper with black or gold calligraphy, hung on doorways during the Lunar New Year to bring luck and ward off evil, featuring two parallel lines with matching structure, number of characters, and contrasting tones, plus a horizontal scroll (横批) above, all expressing wishes for prosperity and happiness
