Chinese New Year Traditions & Activities

The Lunar New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, is the most important holiday in Chinese culture. It is a time for family reunions, honoring ancestors, and ushering in good fortune for the year ahead.

The festival is rooted in a legend about a mythical beast called Nian(Âπ¥). Every year, Nian would emerge to eat livestock and villagers. The people eventually discovered that the beast was terrified of the color red, bright lights, and loud noises. This is why, even today, the holiday is defined by red lanterns, firecrackers, and the rhythmic beating of drums.
