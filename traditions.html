Chinese New Year Traditions & Activities

The Lunar New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, is the most important holiday in Chinese culture. It is a time for family reunions, honoring ancestors, and ushering in good fortune for the year ahead.

The festival is rooted in a legend about a mythical beast called Nian(Âπ¥). Every year, Nian would emerge to eat livestock and villagers.
