5:13 PM Introduction to Peking opera And performer Lesily Lei **邢蕾 (Xing Lei)** a special guest all the way from Vancouver, Canada.
5:15 PM Peking Opera Performance A captivating Peking Opera performance showcasing traditional theater with vibrant costumes and dramatic expressions.
  • 《杨门女将·探谷》 (The Female Generals of the Yang Family - Exploring the Valley)
  • 《捧印》 (Holding the Seal): Mu Guiying bravely takes up the role of general during a national crisis.
  • 《苏三起解》 (Su San's Escort): The story of Su San, a famous courtesan, who is wrongfully imprisoned and laments her fate while being escorted.
5:25 PM Calligraphy Performance. A graceful demonstration of Chinese calligraphy, illustrating the beauty and artistry of traditional writing techniques.
5:40 PM Peking opera Performance:
  • 《凤还巢》 (The Phoenix Returns to the Nest): Cheng Xue'e, the second daughter, refuses to flee with her stepmother to the Zhu family, expressing her determination to maintain her virtue.
  • 《坐宫》 (Sitting in the Palace):
    • The story of Princess Tie Jing of the Liao Dynasty, who helps her husband steal military orders to escape back to Song.
  • 《天女散花》 (Celestial Maiden Scattering Flowers):Describes the sights seen by a celestial maiden traveling from the Land of Fragrance to the city of Vaisali.
5:50:PM Storytelling: The Legend of Chinese New Year. Engaging session about the origins and traditions of Chinese New Year.
6:00 PM Learning Chinese New Year Greetings. An interactive session for kids to learn and practice Chinese New Year greetings. Giving out Red Envelopes: Kids receive red envelopes, an important part of the new year tradition.
6:02 PM Decoration of the Dragon. Opportunity for guests, especially children, to decorate a dragon for the parade.
6:15 PM Dragon Parade Vibrant procession around the event area.
6:30 PM Learning Chinese New Year Greetings again. An interactive session for kids to learn and practice Chinese New Year greetings. Giving out Red Envelopes: Kids receive red envelopes, an important part of the new year tradition.
