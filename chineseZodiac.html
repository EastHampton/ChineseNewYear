Chinese Zodiac & New Year

Chinese New Year (春节)

Chinese New Year is a celebration that transcends simple calendar dates. It is a profound cultural reset, a time to sweep away the ill fortune of the past year and welcome the "new" with open arms loud sounds, and the color red.

The festival is rooted in a legend about a mythical beast called Nian(年). Every year, Nian would emerge to eat livestock and villagers. The people eventually discovered that the beast was terrified of the color red, bright lights, and loud noises. This is why, even today, the holiday is defined by red lanterns, firecrackers, and the rhythmic beating of drums.

The Lunar Cycle and the Great Race

The Chinese Zodiac (生肖) is a 12-year cycle where each year is represented by an animal. According to the legend, the Jade Emperor organized a "Great Race" across a river to determine the order of the zodiac signs. The order they finished the race is the order they appear in the calendar today. Below is a detailed look at the 12 animals of the Chinese Zodiac:

  • The Rat won the race through cunning. He hitched a ride on the Ox's back and jumped off at the finish line.
  • Traits: Clever, quick-witted, resourceful, adaptable
  • Years: 1924, 1936, 1948, 1960, 1972, 1984, 1996, 2008, 2020
  • The Ox was the strongest swimmer and would have won if not for the Rat's trick. Because he was kind enough to carry the Rat across the river, he is forever honored as the second animal.
  • Traits: Strong, reliable, honest, patient, hardworking
  • Years: 1925, 1937, 1949, 1961, 1973, 1985, 1997, 2009, 2021
  • The Tiger came in third. Though powerful, the heavy currents of the river pushed him off course. He used his sheer strength to claw his way to the bank.
  • Traits: Brave, courageous, competitive, passionate
  • Years: 1926, 1938, 1950, 1962, 1974, 1986, 1998, 2010, 2022
  • The Rabbit arrived fourth by hopping across stepping stones. At one point, he slipped but grabbed a floating log that carried him to the shore.
  • Traits: Gentle, peaceful, kind, sympathetic, serene
  • Years: 1927, 1939, 1951, 1963, 1975, 1987, 1999, 2011, 2023
  • The Dragon is the only mythical creature in the zodiac. He would have been first, but he stopped to blow a breath of wind to help the Rabbit and diverted to a nearby village to bring rain to a drought-stricken farm.
  • Traits: Powerful, magnificent, confident, ambitious
  • Years: 1928, 1940, 1952, 1964, 1976, 1988, 2000, 2012, 2024
  • Years: 1929, 1941, 1953, 1965, 1977, 1989, 2001, 2013, 2025
  • The Snake arrived sixth by hiding on the Horse's hoof. When they reached the shore, the Snake wiggled out, startling the Horse and sliding into the sixth spot.
  • Traits: Wise, mysterious, introverted, intelligent
  • The Horse is energetic and free-spirited. After being startled by the Snake, he took the seventh spot.
  • Traits: Energetic, independent, free-spirited, warm
  • Years: 1930, 1942, 1954, 1966, 1978, 1990, 2002, 2014, 2026
  • The Goat, Monkey, and Rooster worked as a team. They found a raft and cleared weeds together to cross the river. The Emperor was so impressed by their teamwork that he granted them the next three spots.
  • Traits: Creative, gentle, warm, artistic, peaceful
  • Years: 1931, 1943, 1955, 1967, 1979, 1991, 2003, 2015, 2027
  • As part of the raft trio, the Monkey used his agility to help guide the craft.
  • Traits: Playful, mischievous, clever, intelligent, witty
  • Years: 1932, 1944, 1956, 1968, 1980, 1992, 2004, 2016, 2028
  • The Rooster found the raft and helped the others stay on course.
  • Traits: Confident, honest, straightforward, hardworking
  • Years: 1933, 1945, 1957, 1969, 1981, 1993, 2005, 2017, 2029
  • The Dog is the best swimmer of the bunch, but he arrived late because he was having too much fun playing in the river's fresh water!
  • Traits: Loyal, faithful, honest, dependable, protective
  • Year: 1934, 1946, 1958, 1970, 1982, 1994, 2006, 2018, 2030
  • The Pig arrived last. Halfway through the race, he got hungry and stopped for a feast, then fell asleep. He woke up just in time to take the final spot.
  • Traits: Generous, kind, compassionate, sincere, diligent
  • Year: 1935, 1947, 1959, 1971, 1983, 1995, 2007, 2019, 2031

How to Celebrate Your Zodiac Year

In Chinese culture, when it is the year of your zodiac sign (本命年), it is actually considered a year of challenges. To ward off bad luck, people often wear red clothing or red accessories (like a red string bracelet) given to them by an elder.
